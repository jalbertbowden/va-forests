2009 Virginia Locality|County Timber Harvest Value and Volume

This chart shows the harvest and stumpage values for the fiscal year July 1, 2009 to June 30, 2010. The numbers are down as would be expected with our economy. The sawtimber numbers are the worse since 1975. The only bright spot was pine pulpwood. However, part of this could be due to the need to purchase more pulwood to make up the difference in loss of residue sawmill chips. Not sure what the impact of BCAP was on totals. Even with the volumes losses, total value managed to improve slightly over last year, due to some weather related spikes in prices. However, much of this value increase was temporary; in general, total harvest values are near the lowest in several years.

In summary, based on forest products tax receipts,

  • Hardwood and pine sawtimber harvest were the smallest since 1975.
  • Hardwood pulpwood harvest lowest since 1991.
  • Total hardwood harvest lowest since 1975.
  • Pine pulpwood volume second best on record to previous year.
  • Have seen significant lost in harvest value in last two years, however this year's value was a little better on lower volume. Indicating that stumpage values did increased some in certain areas.
